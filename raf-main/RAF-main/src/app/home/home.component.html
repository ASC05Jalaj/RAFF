What is RAF?

The Risk Adjustment Factor (RAF) is a sophisticated scoring system that evaluates patient risk based on ICD-10 diagnosis codes, demographic factors, and HCC mappings. This score is fundamental in Medicare Advantage and risk-adjusted payment models, enabling accurate healthcare cost predictions and fair payment allocation.

How Does It Work?

The RAF Calculator employs a systematic approach:

  1. Input ICD-10 diagnosis codes into the system
  2. Automatic mapping to Hierarchical Condition Categories (HCC)
  3. Complex RAF score computation incorporating demographic factors
  4. Identification of potential coexisting conditions (≥70% probability)
  5. Generation of comprehensive total RAF score

RAF Score Calculation

Total RAF score calculation formula:

Total RAF = Σ (HCC RAF Scores) + Demographic Factors

What is an HCC code?
